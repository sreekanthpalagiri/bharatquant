FAQ

Common Questions

Where does BharatQuant get its data? add
Prices are sourced directly from official NSE and BSE Bhavcopy files — the exchange's daily trade data. Fundamental and financial data is fetched from Yahoo Finance and processed through our proprietary pipeline, ensuring accurate F-Score and growth calculations.
What is the Piotroski F-Score? add
The Piotroski F-Score is a 0–9 composite financial health metric developed by Stanford professor Joseph Piotroski. It evaluates a company across nine criteria spanning profitability (e.g. positive ROA), leverage (e.g. lower long-term debt), and operating efficiency (e.g. improving asset turnover). A score of 7–9 identifies fundamentally strong companies often favoured by value investors and institutions.
How does the Excel export work? add
Pro Terminal subscribers can download screener results as a professionally formatted Excel workbook (.xlsx). Each row represents one stock with all 29 columns. Conditional formatting automatically highlights green rows for strong metrics (e.g. F-Score ≥7, ROE >15%) and red for risk indicators (e.g. D/E >2, negative growth), making it easy to scan hundreds of stocks at once.
What is the Penny Stock Shield? add
The Penny Stock Shield automatically filters out stocks trading below a configurable minimum price threshold. This removes manipulated, illiquid, or shell company listings from your screener results — leaving only legitimate, investable candidates. The threshold is set in configuration and applied before any fundamental analysis is run.
